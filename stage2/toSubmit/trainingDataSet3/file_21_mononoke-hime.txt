When an <n>Emishi</n> village is attacked by a fierce demon boar, the young prince <p>Ashitaka</p> puts his life at stake to defend his tribe. With its dying breath, the beast curses the prince's arm, granting him demonic powers while gradually siphoning his life away. Instructed by the village elders to travel westward for a cure, <p>Ashitaka</p> arrives at <n>Tatara</n>, the <n>Iron Town</n>, where he finds himself embroiled in a fierce conflict: <p>Lady Eboshi</p> of <n>Tatara</n>, promoting constant deforestation, stands against <p>Princess San</p> and the sacred spirits of the forest, who are furious at the destruction brought by the humans. As the opposing forces of nature and mankind begin to clash in a desperate struggle for survival, <p>Ashitaka</p> attempts to seek harmony between the two, all the while battling the latent demon inside of him. <p>Princess Mononoke</p> is a tale depicting the connection of technology and nature, while showing the path to harmony that could be achieved by mutual acceptance.
